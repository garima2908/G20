Introduction

The Group of Twenty (G20) is an international forum consisting of 19 individual countries and the European Union. It was established to discuss and coordinate global economic policy and financial stability. Let's learn more about this influential group!

Members of G20

The G20 consists of the following member countries:

  • Argentina
  • Australia
  • Brazil
  • Canada
  • China
  • France
  • Germany
  • India
  • Indonesia
  • Italy
  • Japan
  • South Korea
  • Mexico
  • Russia
  • Saudi Arabia
  • South Africa
  • Turkey
  • United Kingdom
  • United States
  • European Union (EU)

Objectives of G20

The G20 aims to:

  • Promote international financial stability and sustainable economic growth.
  • Address global economic challenges and risks.
  • Facilitate policy coordination between member countries.
  • Discuss issues related to international trade and investments.
  • Address financial regulations and reforms.

Meetings and Summits

The G20 leaders meet annually at the G20 Summit, discussing and making decisions on various economic issues. The host country organizes the summit, and the leaders also engage in bilateral meetings on the sidelines.

Impact of G20

The G20 plays a vital role in shaping global economic policies. Its decisions can significantly impact economies worldwide, affecting trade, investments, and financial regulations.

Conclusion

The G20 is an essential forum that fosters international cooperation and coordination on economic matters. These countries aim to create a more stable and prosperous global economy by working together.
